# Strategy & Decision Frameworks — Detailed Reference

Read this reference when applying strategy, decision-making, or portfolio frameworks.

## SWOT Analysis (Detailed)

### Structure
| | Positive | Negative |
|---|----------|----------|
| **Internal** (controllable) | **Strengths**: Current advantages | **Weaknesses**: Current disadvantages |
| **External** (uncontrollable) | **Opportunities**: Favorable trends | **Threats**: Unfavorable trends |

### Application Rules
1. Start with the business question, not the framework. Ask: "What decision does this SWOT inform?"
2. Each cell should have 3-5 items maximum. Prioritize by impact.
3. Cross-reference: S×O (leverage), W×T (defend), S×T (buffer), W×O (invest).
4. Validate MECE within each cell: no overlaps between Strengths items.

### Common Pitfalls
- Listing features instead of advantages (S) or disadvantages (W).
- Confusing internal (controllable) with external (uncontrollable).
- Creating overly long lists without prioritization.

## 5 Forces Analysis (Detailed)

### Structure
| Force | Key Questions | High = Less Attractive |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|
| Threat of New Entrants | Capital requirements? Regulations? Brand loyalty? | Easy entry |
| Threat of Substitutes | Switching costs? Price-performance of alternatives? | Many substitutes |
| Buyer Power | Concentration? Switching costs? Price sensitivity? | Few large buyers |
| Supplier Power | Concentration? Uniqueness? Switching costs? | Few key suppliers |
| Industry Rivalry | Number of competitors? Growth rate? Differentiation? | Intense competition |

### Application Rules
1. Rate each force as High / Medium / Low with specific evidence.
2. Overall industry attractiveness = weighted combination of 5 forces.
3. Focus on forces that are **changing**, not just current state.

## Decision Matrix (Detailed)

### Structure
1. List options (columns).
2. Define evaluation criteria (rows).
3. Assign weights to criteria (must sum to 100%).
4. Score each option per criteria (1-5 scale).
5. Calculate weighted scores.

### Template
| Criteria | Weight | Option A | Option B | Option C |
|----------|--------|----------|----------|----------|
| [Criteria 1] | 30% | 4 (1.2) | 3 (0.9) | 5 (1.5) |
| [Criteria 2] | 25% | 3 (0.75) | 5 (1.25) | 2 (0.5) |
| [Criteria 3] | 25% | 5 (1.25) | 4 (1.0) | 3 (0.75) |
| [Criteria 4] | 20% | 2 (0.4) | 3 (0.6) | 4 (0.8) |
| **Total** | **100%** | **3.6** | **3.75** | **3.55** |

### Application Rules
1. Define criteria BEFORE evaluating options (avoid confirmation bias).
2. Weights should reflect the business objective, not equal distribution.
3. Perform sensitivity analysis: does the winner change if top weight shifts ±10%?

## BCG Matrix (Detailed)

### Structure (2×2)
| | High Market Growth | Low Market Growth |
|---|---|---|
| **High Relative Market Share** | ⭐ Star: Invest to maintain | 💰 Cash Cow: Harvest |
| **Low Relative Market Share** | ❓ Question Mark: Invest or divest | 🐕 Dog: Divest |

### Application Rules
1. "Relative market share" = your share ÷ largest competitor's share.
2. "Market growth" threshold: typically industry average growth rate.
3. Plot all business units/products on the matrix.
4. Strategy: use Cash Cow profits to fund Stars and promising Question Marks.

## Risk Matrix (Detailed)

### Structure (5×5 or 3×3)
| | Low Impact | Medium Impact | High Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **High Probability** | 🟡 Medium | 🟠 High | 🔴 Critical |
| **Medium Probability** | 🟢 Low | 🟡 Medium | 🟠 High |
| **Low Probability** | 🟢 Low | 🟢 Low | 🟡 Medium |

### Application Rules
1. List all identified risks with description.
2. Assess probability and impact independently.
3. Plot on matrix to determine priority.
4. Define mitigation for 🔴 Critical and 🟠 High risks.
5. Assign owner and review date for each mitigation.
