## Fit for Growth

### Cost Classification
| Category | Definition | Action |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| **Good Cost** | Strengthens differentiating capabilities | Increase investment |
| **Table Stakes** | Needed for operations but not differentiating | Optimize efficiency |
| **Bad Cost** | Low strategic value, legacy overhead | Significantly reduce/eliminate |

### Capability Map

### Application Rules
1. "Good Cost" is the most important category — it's what you INCREASE, not cut.
2. Savings from Bad Cost go to Good Cost (reallocation, not just reduction).
3. Capability system coherence matters — capabilities must reinforce each other.
4. Right-sizing methods: consolidate, outsource, automate, or eliminate.

## TIMM (Total Impact Measurement & Management)

### 4-Dimension Assessment
| Dimension | Positive Impact | Negative Impact | Net |
|-----------|----------------|----------------|-----|
| **Economic** | GDP, jobs, tax | Displacement | [Net] |
| **Social** | Health, education, community | Inequality | [Net] |
| **Environmental** | Biodiversity, clean energy | Emissions, waste | [Net] |
| **Tax** | Corporate, employment, environmental | Tax incentives | [Net] |

### Application Rules
1. Monetize all impacts where possible for comparability.
2. Include indirect and induced effects, not just direct.
3. Document all monetization assumptions — they strongly affect results.
4. Perform sensitivity analysis on key assumptions.
5. Align with GRI/SASB/TCFD reporting standards where applicable.
