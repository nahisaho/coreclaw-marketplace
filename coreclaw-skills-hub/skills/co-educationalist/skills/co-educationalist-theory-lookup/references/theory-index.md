# Education Theory Index — Detailed Reference

Read this reference when applying a specific education theory.

## Learning Theories

### Constructivism (Piaget, Vygotsky)
- **Key idea**: Learners actively construct knowledge through experience.
- **Application**: Design activities where students discover patterns, not just receive information.
- **Pitfall**: Pure discovery without scaffolding can overwhelm novice learners.

### Behaviorism (Skinner, Pavlov)
- **Key idea**: Learning is observable behavior change through reinforcement.
- **Application**: Use immediate feedback, positive reinforcement, clear expectations.
- **Pitfall**: Overreliance on extrinsic rewards can undermine intrinsic motivation.

### Social Learning Theory (Bandura)
- **Key idea**: Learning through observation, imitation, and modeling.
- **Application**: Teacher modeling, peer demonstrations, worked examples.
- **Pitfall**: Negative behaviors are also learned through modeling.

### Connectivism (Siemens)
- **Key idea**: Knowledge is distributed across networks; learning is navigating those networks.
- **Application**: Collaborative projects, networked learning, digital literacy.
- **Pitfall**: Information overload without curation skills.

## Motivation Theories

### Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan)
- **3 needs**: Autonomy, Competence, Relatedness.
- **Application**: Give choice (autonomy), calibrate challenge (competence), build community (relatedness).
- **Pitfall**: Forcing autonomy on unprepared learners increases anxiety.

### Growth Mindset (Dweck)
- **Key idea**: Intelligence is malleable, not fixed.
- **Application**: Praise effort/strategy, use "not yet" framing, normalize mistakes.
- **Pitfall**: Superficial adoption ("just try harder") without structural support is ineffective.

### Flow Theory (Csikszentmihalyi)
- **Key idea**: Optimal experience when challenge matches skill level.
- **Application**: Adjust task difficulty to maintain engagement zone.
- **Pitfall**: Too easy = boredom, too hard = anxiety.

## Assessment Theories

### Constructive Alignment (Biggs)
- **Key idea**: Objectives ↔ Activities ↔ Assessment must be aligned.
- **Application**: Design assessment before activities; ensure all three match.
- **Pitfall**: Misalignment is the most common cause of ineffective instruction.

### Bloom's Taxonomy (Anderson & Krathwohl, revised)
- **6 levels**: Remember → Understand → Apply → Analyze → Evaluate → Create.
- **Application**: Annotate every assessment item with its level; distribute across levels.
- **Pitfall**: Overweighting "Remember" level in assessments.

### Formative Assessment (Black & Wiliam)
- **Key idea**: Assessment FOR learning (not OF learning).
- **Application**: Exit tickets, self-assessment, peer feedback during learning.
- **Pitfall**: Using formative data for grading defeats its purpose.

## Developmental Theories

### Cognitive Development (Piaget)
- **Stages**: Sensorimotor → Preoperational → Concrete operational → Formal operational.
- **Application**: Match instruction complexity to cognitive stage.

### Psychosocial Development (Erikson)
- **8 stages**: Each with a crisis to resolve.
- **Application**: Understand the developmental task at each age for appropriate support.

### Moral Development (Kohlberg)
- **3 levels**: Pre-conventional → Conventional → Post-conventional.
- **Application**: Frame behavioral guidance at the student's moral reasoning level.

### Zone of Proximal Development (Vygotsky)
- **Key idea**: Gap between independent ability and assisted ability.
- **Application**: Provide scaffolding in the ZPD; gradually release responsibility.

## Instructional Design

### Universal Design for Learning (CAST)
- **3 principles**: Multiple means of Engagement, Representation, Action/Expression.
- **Application**: Offer choices in how students access, engage with, and demonstrate learning.

### PBIS (Sugai & Horner)
- **3 tiers**: Universal (all) → Targeted (some) → Intensive (few).
- **Application**: Multi-tier behavioral support system.
